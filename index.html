Neha Simha

Currently, I work with the Chen Lab on understanding stigmatizing language in clinical notes for pregnancy patients. Before that, I worked at Stanford University with Dr. Rishi Raj in the Interstitial Lung Disease (ILD) program, analyzing clinical data to develop machine learning models to predict patient progression. I also collaborated with Dr. Curt Langlotz at the Center for Artificial Intelligence in Medicine and Imaging.

I graduated from UC Berkeley with a B.A. in Molecular and Cell Biology, Immunology. During undergrad, I was a research apprentice at the Scott Lab, working with Dr. Philip Shiu and Professor Kristin Scott on understanding interactions between taste modalities in the fruit fly brain. My experiences also include volunteering at health clinics, sparking my passion for equitable healthcare access.
