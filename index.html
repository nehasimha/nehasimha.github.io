Neha Simha

I am a researcher working at the intersection of artificial intelligence and medicine. Most recently, I worked at Stanford University with Dr. Rishi Raj in the Interstitial Lung Disease (ILD) program, analyzing clinical data to develop a machine learning model to predict patient prognosis. I also collaborated with Dr. Curt Langlotz in the Center for Artificial Intelligence in Medicine and Imaging.

I graduated from UC Berkeley in 2022 with a B.A. in Molecular and Cell Biology, Immunology. During undergrad, I was a research apprentice at the Scott Lab, working with postdoctoral scholar Philip Shiu and Professor Kristin Scott on understanding interactions between taste modalities in the fruit fly brain. I also took upper division public health classes and volunteered at health clinics, sparking a passion in equitable healthcare access.

Teaching & Volunteering

I volunteer once a week at the San Francisco Free Clinic, where we provide free medical care to uninsured patients, including diabetes management, imaging referrals, and minor procedures. I am passionate about contributing to a future where quality care is available to everyone, regardless of their insurance status.

Since 2024, I have taught part-time at Inspirit AI, facilitating the Scholars curriculum for elementary and high school students (in-person at Khan Lab School and California High School as well as virtually). I have also taught the Deep Dives course on AI/ML applications in medicine and mentor students one-on-one.
